\section{Contributions}

Our work demonstrates that effect handlers can be used to implement software contracts, proven by proving a theoretical compiler, and an accompanying proof of concept in OCaml. Our OCaml implementation is available on Github\footnote{\href{https://github.com/nganhkhoa/conteff}{https://github.com/nganhkhoa/conteff}}.

The rest of the thesis is organized as follows. In Chapter \ref{chap:contracts} and Chapter \ref{chap:effects} we introduced the formal languages of Contracts as the source language and Effects as the target language after compilation. In Chapter \ref{chap:compiler}, we discuss a theoretical compiler on the formal languages. The OCaml language is introduced in Chapter \ref{chap:ocaml} and an implementation of the compiler is detailed in Chapter \ref{chap:implementation}. Finally, we conclude our remarks in Chapter \ref{chap:final}.
